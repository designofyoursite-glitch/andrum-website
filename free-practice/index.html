Why this works

You cannot think your way out of a body alarm.

When relationship anxiety hits, your body can move faster than your logic. Your heart rate changes. Your breath gets shallow. Your attention locks onto the phone.

That is why the practice starts with the body, not with advice. A guided breathing rhythm gives your nervous system a different signal first. Then you may have enough space to decide what actually needs to happen next.

Andrum

Made for anxious attachment moments, not generic stress.

Andrum SOS Practices are short guided breathwork audios for the moments when close relationships hit the nervous system hard: silence, jealousy, shame, racing thoughts, freeze, anger, and the desperate urge to reach out.

The free practice is the first one: anxiety after a message or conversation. Start there.

Andrum practices are body-based self-support tools, not therapy, medical care, or crisis support. If breathwork feels uncomfortable, return to natural breathing and pause. If you feel unsafe or at risk of harming yourself, contact local emergency support or a mental health professional.
